The BIW gap

Bath Iron Works shows only $25 M of visible subs against $8.8 B of prime obligation — an FFATA reporting gap, not an absence of subcontracting. The true BIW supplier footprint is invisible to USAspending.

Capped values

Subaward pairs exceeding 1× prime contract value are capped at the prime; pairs exceeding 2× prime are excluded as clear data corruption (e.g., the NASSCO Cowpens phantom $920 M).

KEY FINDINGS
Six material shifts from the prior analysis.
From the v3 subaward re-pull, 10 April 2026.
01

Raytheon overtakes BIW as the #2 prime.

Seven FY20–26 Standard Missile production contracts worth $5.56 B were missed by the original keyword pull. Surfaced via a Navy agency-code sweep with a $100 M dollar floor.

▴ NEW OBLIGATION + $5.56 B
02

Marinette was never $47 billion.

USAspending issues up to 12 cumulative-style snapshots per subaward. Summing them produced a $47.17 B phantom on the LM LCS Freedom prime. Verified: $2.49 B across 23 distinct subcontracts.

▾ CORRECTION 19× lower
03

HII LPD 28 is 97% pre-window.

The Fort Lauderdale prime obligated $3.00 B before 2020 but only $77 M inside the FY20–26 window. Cumulative-vs-window confusion inflated the prior estimate by ~$6 B on LPDs alone.

04

BIW under-reports subawards entirely.

~$8.8 B of DDG-51 prime obligation produced only $51 M of reported subawards across all six BIW PIIDs — an FFATA compliance gap, not an absence of subcontracting.

▾ FFATA GAP 0.6% visible
05

The "hidden RF backbone" was overstated 10×.

CAES + Mercury combined exposure across SPY-6, SEWIP Block 2, and SLQ-32(V)6 is $503 M — not the $5 B+ implied by summed cumulative-snapshot records.

06

DDG-1000 is in closeout.

Only $1.35 B of window obligation across 7 PIIDs. The BIW lead-shipbuilder ($3.31 B) and Raytheon mission systems integrator ($1.34 B) are 100% pre-window.

§ METHOD

Per-mod sum, not cumulative.

Window deltas are computed as the per-mod sum of FPDS obligatedAmount (this action only) across all in-window mods — not cumulative-since-contract-award. The prior version of this analysis used cumulative figures, which alone overstated FY20–26 obligation by roughly $12 B on DDG-51 new construction (pre-window MYP leakage of the FY13–17 and FY18–22 Block Buys).

Subawards were re-pulled from USAspending /api/v2/subawards across all 170 in-scope PIIDs and deduplicated in three stages: (i) per sub_id MAX to collapse cumulative snapshots; (ii) identical-amount duplicates across mod boundaries; (iii) cap at 1.0× prime contract value, and exclude pairs exceeding 2× prime as clear data corruption.
