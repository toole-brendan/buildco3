# US Navy Taxonomy of Work on In-Service Ships

## Key Finding: The Navy Does NOT Use "MRO" as a Single Category

The US Navy does not frame work on existing ships as a monolithic "Maintenance, Repair, and Operations" (MRO) category. Instead, the official framework separates work into **distinct top-level categories** with specific definitions, funding streams, and governance structures. The two master categories are **Maintenance** and **Modernization**, which are explicitly defined as separate programs:

> "The Navy's program for ship material readiness has two separate components: ship maintenance and ship modernization. While maintenance and modernization programs and budgets are distinct, their planning and execution are intertwined."
> — OPNAVINST 4700.7N, Section 103d, p. 1-2

---

## I. MAINTENANCE (Restoring/Sustaining Material Condition)

Maintenance is work that **restores or sustains** a ship's existing design condition. It is organized along three independent axes: **level**, **trigger/type**, and **depth**.

### A. By Maintenance Level (Who Does It)

| Level | Performer | Scope |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| **Organizational** | Ship's crew (Ship's Force) | Inspecting, servicing, lubricating, adjusting; repair/replacement of parts, minor assemblies. "First line of defense." |
| **Intermediate** | Fleet Maintenance Activities (IMAs, tenders, Regional Maintenance Centers) | Calibration, repair, refurbishment of components; emergency manufacture of parts; alteration installation. Requires higher skill than organizational but below depot. |
| **Depot / Industrial** | Naval shipyards, private shipyards under contract | Major overhaul, complete rebuild of parts/assemblies/end-items; manufacture of parts; modifications, testing, reclamation. Requires specialized facilities, tooling, and journeyman-level skills. |

**Sources:** OPNAVINST 4700.7N, Chapters 4-6 (Sections 401, 501, 601), pp. 4-1, 5-1, 6-1; JFMM Rev D, Vol II Part I Ch 2 Section 2.2, pp. 367-368

### B. By Trigger / Type (Why and When It Happens)

| Type | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Preventive / Planned Maintenance** | Actions intended to prevent or discover functional failures, scheduled on a recurring basis. |
| **Time-Directed Maintenance** | Performed periodically regardless of equipment condition, based on elapsed time or recurrent operations. |
| **Condition-Based Maintenance (CBM)** | Performed only when there is objective evidence of actual or predictable failure, using sensor data and external measurements. |
| **Condition-Directed Preventive Maintenance** | Periodic diagnostic tests/inspections comparing existing condition against established standards, to discover potential failures before they occur. |
| **Corrective Maintenance** | Actions performed as a result of failure, to return/restore equipment to acceptable performance. |
| **Emergent Maintenance** | Conducted with little or no notice to restore a failed mission-essential system. Normally related to CASREPs. |
| **Life Cycle Maintenance** | Scheduled per the Class Maintenance Plan (CMP), supported by Mandatory Technical Requirements (MTRs). |

**Sources:** OPNAVINST 4700.7N, Section 103d, Section 201.3, Appendix B definitions #4-6, #10, #18, #55, #61, #68 (pp. 1-2, 2-1, B-1 through B-10); JFMM Rev D, Vol II Part I Ch 2 Sections 2.3-2.4, pp. 368-369

### C. By Depth of Repair (How Far It Goes)

The JFMM defines **Classes of Overhaul** that describe the authorized scope on any given system or component:

| Class | Name | Scope |
|-------|------|-------|
| **Class A** | Return to "like new" | Restore to like-new condition AND upgrade to most recent design/technical specifications. Blurs line with modernization. |
| **Class B** | Return to original performance | Restore to original performance profile. Provide high assurance of function until next scheduled overhaul. No modifications. |
| **Class C** | Specific repair | Correct particular deficiencies or malfunctions. Synonymous with "repair." |
| **Class D** | Inspect and report | "Open, Inspect, and Report" — diagnostic only. Various inspections followed by a report identifying required repairs. |

**Source:** JFMM FY28, Vol VII Ch 4 Appendix E, Section 2 para 2-1 (p. VII-4E-6)

### D. By Maintenance Strategy (Ship Class Approach)

Different ship classes follow different overarching maintenance strategies:

| Code | Strategy | Description |
|------|----------|-------------|
| **EOC** | Engineered Operating Cycle | Structured engineered approach minimizing time in depot availabilities |
| **IMP** | Incremental Maintenance Plan | Series of incremental depot maintenance actions (carriers) |
| **PM** | Phased Maintenance | Short, labor-intensive availabilities in lieu of overhauls |
| **PROG** | Progressive Maintenance | For ships with reduced manning and limited organizational-level capability |

**Source:** OPNAVINST 4700.7N, Table 11-1, p. 11-4

### E. Class Maintenance Plan (CMP) Task Types

| Task Type | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **I-tasks** | Material condition assessment tasks |
| **Q-tasks** | Qualified repair and life renewal tasks |
| **R-tasks** | Availability routine tasks |
| **Ship change tasks** | Authorized Fleet and Program ship change tasks |

**Source:** JFMM Rev D, Vol II Part II Ch 1, p. ~780

## II. MODERNIZATION (Changing the Ship — NOT Maintenance)

Modernization is explicitly **not** maintenance. It is a separate program with separate budgets:

> "The ship modernization program is designed to either add new capability or improve reliability and maintainability of existing systems."
> — OPNAVINST 4700.7N, Section 103d(2), p. 1-3

> "Modernization encompasses replacing obsolete equipment or systems, increasing capacity beyond installed design or adding new capabilities."
> — JFMM Rev D, p. ~2062

### A. Two Types of Ship Changes

| Type | Authority | Funding | Purpose |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Program Changes** | System Commands / Program Executive Offices (NAVSEA) | SYSCOM funded | Add new capabilities, major system upgrades |
| **Fleet Changes** | Type Commander (TYCOM) | TYCOM ship maintenance funding | Safety of personnel/equipment, increased efficiency/reliability/maintainability. Generally do NOT add new capability. |

**Source:** JFMM Rev D, Vol II Part I Ch 2 Section 2.6, p. ~374

### B. Alteration Types

| Type | Abbreviation | Description |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Ship Alteration** | SHIPALT | General ship modifications |
| **Machinery Alteration** | MACHALT | Machinery system modifications |
| **Ordnance Alteration** | ORDALT | Weapons/ordnance system modifications |
| **Strategic Systems Programs Alteration** | SPALT | Strategic weapons modifications |
| **Temporary Alteration** | TEMPALT | Reversible modifications |
| **Temporary Modification** | TEMPMOD | Temporary changes |
| **Alteration Equivalent to Repair (AER)** | AER | Looks like a modification but treated as repair-authorized. Funded from maintenance accounts. |

**Sources:** JFMM Rev D, pp. ~110, ~374; JFMM FY28, Vol VII Ch 4 Appendix E, Section 5 para 5-5 (p. VII-4E-16)

### C. Modernization in Depot Contract Specifications

When modernization is executed during depot availabilities, it uses **Class E** overhaul standard:

> "Requirements necessary to support alterations, with much of the direction coming from the Ship Installation Drawing (SID) Package for the alteration."
> — JFMM FY28, Vol VII Ch 4 Appendix E, para 2-1.5

### D. The Maintenance-Modernization Boundary

The Navy enforces a hard boundary between repair and modernization:

> "Repair oriented Work Item Specifications must not alter the military characteristics of a ship in any manner."
> — JFMM FY28, Vol VII Ch 4 Appendix E, para 5-5

However, there is an acknowledged gray zone — **modernization in lieu of repair**:

> "Cost-benefit analysis and operational schedules may identify modernization as the best alternative to restoring capability vice corrective maintenance (i.e., modernization in lieu of repair)."
> — OPNAVINST 4700.7N, Section 103d, p. 1-2

And **Fleet Alterations** (fleet-funded modernization) explicitly bridge the two programs:

> "Fleet alterations are programmed and budgeted as part of the maintenance requirement. Fleet-funded modernization does not normally increase or add new capabilities. Fleet alterations should address safety of either personnel or equipment or both, reduce total ownership cost, or replace obsolete equipment."
> — OPNAVINST 4700.7N, Section 1102.7, p. 11-6

## III. SERVICES (Support Tasks)

A fourth category of work recognized on the CSMP alongside maintenance and modernization:

> Services are "overhead items (force protection, berthing ashore, cranes, etc.) in support of maintenance availabilities."
> — JFMM Rev D, p. ~2062

## IV. AVAILABILITY TYPES (The Execution Vehicles)

All maintenance and modernization work is executed during **availabilities** — scheduled periods when a ship is available for work. These are not work types themselves but the scheduling framework.

### A. CNO-Scheduled Availabilities (Major — >6 months)

| Type | Abbreviation | Key Feature |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| Regular Overhaul | ROH | Standard overhaul for certain ship classes |
| Complex Overhaul | COH | For nuclear-powered ships |
| Engineered Overhaul | EOH | For certain ship classes |
| Refueling Overhaul | — | Nuclear refueling + overhaul |
| Refueling Complex Overhaul | RCOH | Nuclear carrier refueling |
| Engineered Refueling Overhaul | ERO | Nuclear ship refueling |
| Depot Modernization Period | DMP | Primarily for high-priority alterations |
| Planned Incremental Availability | PIA | Carriers in IMP, ≤6 months |
| Docking Planned Incremental Availability | DPIA | PIA with dry-docking |
| Carrier Incremental Availability | CIA | Depot maintenance + modernization on carriers |
| Post Shakedown Availability | PSA | After new construction/conversion shakedown |

### B. CNO-Scheduled Availabilities (Minor — <6 months)

| Type | Abbreviation | Key Feature |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| Selected Restricted Availability | SRA | For PROG or EOC ships |
| Docking SRA | DSRA | SRA with dry-docking |
| Extended SRA | ESRA | SRA beyond normal scope |
| Extended Docking SRA | EDSRA | DSRA beyond normal scope |
| Phased Maintenance Availability | PMA | Short, labor-intensive |
| Docking PMA | DPMA | PMA with dry-docking |
| Extended Docking PMA | EDPMA | DPMA beyond normal scope |
| Extended Refit Period | ERP | 4-6 months, SSBNs/SSGNs |
| Service Craft Overhaul | SCO | Non-docking, service craft |
| Docking Service Craft Overhaul | DSCO | Docking, service craft |
| Surface Incremental Availability | SIA | Non-docking, FDNF surface ships |
| Interim Dry-Docking | IDD | Hull-specific, extends operating cycle |
| Pre-Inactivation Restricted Availability | PIRA | Final availability before decommissioning |
| Inactivation Availability | INAC | Prepare ship for disposal |

### C. Non-CNO-Scheduled Availabilities (Fleet Maintenance)

| Type | Abbreviation | Key Feature |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| Continuous Maintenance Availability | CMAV | 2-6 weeks, once per non-deployed quarter |
| Ship's Force Upkeep | — | Ship self-maintenance period |
| Major Maintenance Period | MMP | SSGNs, 15-17 weeks |
| Unscheduled CM Availability | — | Yearlong catch-all for non-emergent work |
| Emergent Availability | — | Urgent work, accepts disruption risk |
| Voyage Repair | VR | Corrective maintenance for deployment-critical items only |
| ERATA | — | Emergent restricted/technical availabilities, little or no notice |
| ORATA | — | Miscellaneous IMA/depot repair programs, centrally funded |

**Sources:** OPNAVINST 4700.7N, Chapters 7-8, Section 1102, Appendix B; JFMM Rev D, Vol II Part I Ch 3 Section 3.2, p. ~413; Ch 4 Section 4.3, p. ~676

## V. END-OF-LIFE WORK

Two specific availability types handle ships leaving service:

- **Pre-Inactivation Restricted Availability (PIRA)**: Final abbreviated operational cycle before inactivation
- **Inactivation Availability (INAC)**: Prepare a ship for inactivation or disposal; scope depends on planned disposition

**Source:** OPNAVINST 4700.7N, Appendix B #30, #54

## VI. COMPLETE WORK TAXONOMY SUMMARY

```
WORK ON IN-SERVICE NAVAL VESSELS
│
├── MAINTENANCE (restore/sustain existing design condition)
│   ├── By Level
│   │   ├── Organizational (Ship's Force)
│   │   ├── Intermediate (IMAs, tenders, RMCs)
│   │   └── Depot / Industrial (shipyards)
│   ├── By Trigger
│   │   ├── Planned / Preventive
│   │   │   ├── Time-Directed
│   │   │   ├── Condition-Based (CBM/CBM+)
│   │   │   └── Condition-Directed
│   │   ├── Corrective (failure-driven repair)
│   │   └── Emergent (urgent, mission-essential)
│   ├── By Depth (Classes of Overhaul)
│   │   ├── Class A — Like-new + upgrade to latest specs
│   │   ├── Class B — Original performance, carry to next overhaul
│   │   ├── Class C — Specific deficiency repair
│   │   └── Class D — Inspect and report only
│   └── By Strategy (ship-class level)
│       ├── Engineered Operating Cycle (EOC)
│       ├── Incremental Maintenance Plan (IMP)
│       ├── Phased Maintenance (PM)
│       └── Progressive Maintenance (PROG)
│
├── MODERNIZATION (add capability or change the ship)
│   ├── Program Changes (SYSCOM/PEO funded — new capabilities)
│   ├── Fleet Changes (TYCOM funded — safety/efficiency/obsolescence)
│   ├── Alteration Types: SHIPALT, MACHALT, ORDALT, SPALT, TEMPALT, TEMPMOD
│   ├── Alteration Equivalent to Repair (AER) — hybrid
│   ├── Modernization in Lieu of Repair — hybrid
│   └── Class E overhaul standard (supports alteration installation)
│
├── SERVICES (support tasks during availabilities)
│   └── Force protection, berthing, cranes, scaffolding, etc.
│
└── END-OF-LIFE
    ├── Pre-Inactivation Restricted Availability (PIRA)
    └── Inactivation Availability (INAC)
```

## VII. FOUR CSMP WORK ITEM CATEGORIES

When individual work items are written in the Current Ship's Maintenance Project (CSMP), they fall into exactly four categories:

1. **Condition Based Maintenance** — "the art of managing material failures" (degraded equipment)
2. **Engineered Maintenance** — "the art of anticipating failure" (CMP/PMS-driven)
3. **Modernization** — "replacing obsolete equipment or systems, increasing capacity beyond installed design or adding new capabilities"
4. **Services** — "overhead items in support of maintenance availabilities"

**Source:** JFMM Rev D, p. ~2062
